# History 

The school was established in March 1992 on the initiative of E. I. Hurvits, who at that time was the head of the Zhovtnevyi district of Odesa and rightly believed that the building at 3 Sabanieiv Mist, unique in its beauty, architecture, and history, should belong to the young generation of Odesans, who together with their teachers would turn it into a true Temple of Art.

Thus, the Center for Aesthetic Education of Children and Youth appeared under the leadership of director Alla Yevheniivna Pivniak, and the first steps were taken on the difficult path of developing children's creativity.

The glorious traditions of theatrical Odesa and the absence of educational institutions with a theatrical profile in the city prompted the school's teachers to direct the development of the educational institution specifically in this direction.

Thus, in December 1996, under the leadership of director Vladyslava Mykolaivna Sosytska, a new "School of Arts No. 1 with a theatrical profile" appeared, which retained its variety and art departments within its walls. This made it possible, alongside theatrical projects, to open exhibitions of young artists and famous masters for their native Odesa in the "Mist" (Bridge) art gallery (directed by T. S. Mykhailychenko), as well as the super-popular TV show and festival "Zirochka" (Little Star) (artistic director – O. S. Stepanov).

A new turn in the creative history of this educational institution was 2003, which brought the building on Sabanieiv Mist a new status and a new name: "Children's Theatrical School of Odesa" (DTS).

And since 2005, with the arrival of the new director Olena Borysivna Tkach, DTS has been actively developing new projects – cooperating with the National Union of Theater Artists (NSTDU), the V. Vasylko Ukrainian Theater, the Odesa Puppet Theater, the O. V. Bleshchunov Municipal Museum of Personal Collections, the Odesa branch of the "Intellectual Forum of Ukraine" and other creative organizations. DTS and the Creative Association "Diadia Fedir" (Uncle Fedir) were the founders of the International Art Festivals "Odeskyi Tramvaichyk" (Odesa Tram) and "Karpatska Vesna" (Carpathian Spring), providing methodological, creative, and practical assistance to various art projects.

In 2011, a very important event for all of us took place – DTS moved to a new address – 24 Sofiivska Street. Thanks to the efforts of caring people – the then Vice-Mayor of Odesa O. O. Pavlova, Deputy Head of the Department of Culture and Arts L. M. Kryzhalko, Odesa patron and businessman D. Ya. Shpynarov, the school solved its most painful issue – the "housing" one. We are confident that in the new building, DTS will reach new creative heights! Here is what the mass media wrote about this event: "The Children's Theatrical School moved to the premises on Sofiivska Street in 2011, and in a fairly short time managed to almost completely settle in and establish the educational process. Currently, the school's concert hall is undergoing additional equipping with lighting fixtures. By the beginning of the school year, the institution's administration managed to master and carry out serious repair work in the premises of the building that had not been used before. Now, additional classrooms and a costume room are located here."

In 2020, on the eve of its 25th anniversary, "Teatralo4ka" (Little Theater) received a new status and name – Communal Institution of Primary Specialized Education "Art Theatrical School of Odesa". This is one of the conditions of the art schools' reform, according to the changes in the Law on Education. Frankly speaking, we were not very happy about it, because it seems to us that cultural officials "overdid it" and came up with a not entirely correct name. What else can a theatrical school be, if not an art school? It would have been more correct this way: "School of Theatrical Art". 

And in 2025, in connection with the decision of the city council, the current name appeared – **Odesa Theatrical School**. But nevertheless – our traditional "chant": "A! A! DTSha!" remained with us forever.

Since 2009, with the support of the State Methodological Center under the Ministry of Culture of Ukraine, our school has been conducting All-Ukrainian theatrical seminars-workshops for teachers of theatrical schools, theatrical departments of specialized creative educational institutions, heads, and methodologists of cultural institutions. The programs of these seminars are diverse and very necessary, because, following the training, all participants receive an official document on professional development.

Until 2016, they were held at the end of March in a biennial mode (once every 2 years) and were dedicated to International Theater Day, which is why they received the symbolic name – "Spring. Odesa. Theater".

And since 2018, "Teatralo4ka" has been holding these seminars at the end of the theatrical season and at the beginning of the admission campaign – in June. And it is clear why! A large number of our graduates enter the creative higher education institutions (HEIs) of the country both after the 9th and 11th grades. In response to such a creative request, a unique theatrical project appeared – the open festival of applicants to creative educational institutions "Odesa.Theater.PRO" with the support of NSTDU.

The main idea of this festival is that applicants who dream of becoming actors, directors, media professionals, and specialists in other creative professions can meet with the masters who recruit first-year students in various creative universities in Ukraine. During the first three years of the festival, the school hosted representatives from 18 Ukrainian cities and towns. Out of 72 participants in these first three fests, 44 became students of creative educational institutions in different cities and countries. Unfortunately, recent years have been sadly marked by heavy losses of our beautiful colleagues and wonderful teachers – **S. M. Imas, N. A. Baldin, I. M. Kaplia**. We always remember and keep in our hearts the memories of these beautiful people who raised hundreds of talented students.

The school is proud of its brilliant students who annually conquer the juries of festivals and competitions of all levels.

Our children also successfully show themselves on the professional theatrical stage, participating in the plays "Cherry Wind", "Burnt by the Sun", "The City of My Childhood", "Family Stories" (Ukrainian Theater), "To Catch Up in Limpopo", and "Odesa-Mama" (Theater of Musical Comedy). You will also find the names of young actors – students of the school in the credits of the movies "To Catch Kaidash", "Hell's Banner, or Cossack Christmas", "Hero's Star", "Steep", "By the River", "Stronger Than Fire", "Island of Surprises", "Season of Discoveries", "Liquidation", Ukrainian series "Servant of the People", "Papik", "Central Hospital", etc.

All fans of the TV program "The Smartest" know our **Maksym Nakonechnyi** as the most noble winner of this game, and 15 years later in 2022, the film "Butterfly Vision" by this young Ukrainian film director represented Ukraine at the Cannes Film Festival.

In 2015, our graduate **Arina Postolova** starred in Eva Neymann's film "Song of Songs" and became the owner of two "Golden DUKes" at the VI Odesa International Film Festival. And the list of graduates who entered creative universities or have already realized themselves in the profession as actors, directors of feature and documentary films, TV producers and presenters, artists, designers, musicians – continues to expand every year, and their number has already exceeded a hundred!

Performances and concerts of the school's students have become a long-awaited holiday in the "House with an Angel", and in Children's Shelter No. 1, and in the Children's Shelter "Way Home", and in the sanatorium for war veterans on the Fountain, kindergartens, libraries, in the unit of military sailors, for soldiers undergoing rehabilitation in the Odesa hospital, and simply in Odesa schools. And who do you think accompanied the mayor of Odesa in the costumes of the founding fathers of the city at the celebration of Odesa's Birthday? Who marched cheerfully to Dumskaya Square to the main Christmas tree of Odesa as part of the New Year's carnival? Who met Sir Oleg Filimonov on the deck of the white liner of the Odesa Humorina? Our children, of course!

Our children, who so imperceptibly turn into graduates, into successful people, into colleagues from the creative workshop, who do not forget their – *alma mater* – the Odesa Theatrical School.

A wonderful tradition of our school has become the **"Educational Theater"**, which unites graduates of different years and is a creative platform for students of the basic acting course and the "Acting School" for adults.

In April 2009, the play "Myna Mazailo" took part in the International Theatrical Festival "Southern Mask" (Mykolaiv) and won a diploma in the nomination "Creative Discovery of the Festival".

And in September 2009, the play "Oblom-off" won first place at the International Theatrical Festival "Live!" in Crimea.

The plays "The Maids" and "Want to Sleep" received the first prizes of the Odesa festivals "Golden Mask of Shakespeare" and "No Tickets Left", respectively.

A traditional event of the "Graduates' Theater" is the preparation of the "Initiation into Art" holiday, which takes place every year in October for new students of the school. Since 2005, the scriptwriter and director of this event has been the director-teacher F. F. Tkach. It is nice that in recent years young directors-teachers **S. A. Nadopta, H. F. Komadovska, P. V. Koshka** have actively joined in writing the script. 

Actively developing and accepting the challenges of recent years, since 2012 the school has created a completely new structure of the theatrical department, developed by many years of collective creative experience. The success of this methodology is confirmed by our graduates, who after studying at school, as we say – "go into the profession" and glorify their school, their "Teatralo4ka", their creative *alma mater*.

We can proudly state that the Odesa Theatrical School has become a truly professional educational institution and has firmly occupied the niche of a basic school of primary theatrical education in Ukraine!
